pharmacodynamics

Diazepam binds to the GABAA receptor, increasing GABA’s affinity for the receptor, which allows chloride ions to flow into neurons. This hyperpolarizes the neurons, making it harder for them to reach the threshold for action potential, thus reducing seizure activity.

Pharmacokinetic Parameters

Absorption: Diazepam has 90%oral bioavailability and 97%intranasal .
Distribution: The volume of distribution of diazepam at steady state is 0.8-1 L/kg. The protein bound of diazepam is 98% (PO) 95-98% (intranasal.
Metabolism: It is rapidlly Metabolized by hepatic P450 enzymes CYP2C19, CYP3A4
Active metabolites: N-desmethyldiazepam and temazepam, which are both further metabolized to oxazepam; largely eliminated by glucuronidation
Elimination: The Half-life of diazepam is 20-70 hr (active metabolite); 49.2 hr (10-mg intranasal dose). It’s renal clearance is 20-30 mL/min.
Excretion: Diazepam is primarily excreted by urine .

Contraindications

Diazepam is contraindicated in mysthenia gravis .
It is also contraindicated in conditions like hypersensitivity.

Use in Pregnancy & Nursing

Diazepam is a Pregnancy category D drug . Breastfeeding is not recommended in patients receiving diazepam .Special care must be taken when diazepam is used during labor and delivery.

Warning & Precautions

Advise both patients and caregivers about risks of respiratory depression and sedation when diazepam is used with opioids&Alcohol .
Use caution in COPD, sleep apnea, renal/hepatic disease, open-angle glaucoma (questionable), depression, , history of drug abuse.
