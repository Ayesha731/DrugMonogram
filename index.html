Overview

Introduction

Diazepam is an anxiolytic drug belonging to the benzodiazepine class. It has a rapid onset and is commonly used to treat panic disorders, severe anxiety, alcohol withdrawal, and seizures.

Advise both patients and caregivers about the risks of respiratory depression and sedation when diazepam is used with opioids & alcohol.

üîÅ Drug-Drug Interaction

Diazepam interacts with opioid-class drugs such as: Morphine, Codeine, Hydrocodone, and Cannabis. These combinations can increase the risk of severe sedation, respiratory depression, and even death.
